Scholarship Finder

Zell Miller Scholarship

The Zell Miller Scholarship provides tuition assistance to applicants interested in attending a qualifying secondary school in Georgia. Recipients have over 50 public, private, and technical schools to choose from.

  • Georgia residency, U.S. citizenship, high school diploma or GED, minimum 3.0 GPA, and meeting certain academic achievement standards.
  • Up to $5,600 per year
  • Varies depending on the institution

Army ROTC Scholarships

TArmy ROTC scholarships are available for high school and college students, as well as enlisted soldiers. They provide tuition assistance, living stipends, book, uniform, and other fee help, and possible promotion within ranks upon graduation.

  • High school or college student, U.S. citizen, between ages 17 and 26, meet physical and mental fitness standards, and agree to serve in the military after graduation.
  • Full tuition and fees, plus monthly stipend
  • December 1 for high school students, October 1 for college students

No Essay Scholarships

The most popular no essay scholarship is offered by Niche. It awards students with $2,000.

  • None, simply complete an online application.
  • $2,000
  • Ongoing, awarded quarterly

Presidential Scholarship

The Presidential Scholarship is awarded to students with a 3.00 or higher GPA at the University of Akron.

  • 3.00 or higher GPA, automatic consideration through application for admission.
  • Varies, up to full tuition
  • Automatic consideration through application for admission

Gates Scholarship

The Gates Scholarship seeks to provide tuition assistance for minority students who come from low-income families, by covering tuition fees that will not be covered by families and financial aid.

  • Minority student, low-income family, Pell Grant recipient, 3.00 or higher GPA, planning to pursue a degree in a STEM field.
  • Up to $200,000 over four years
  • September 15
